The Rich History of Odisha

From ancient Kalinga to modern Odisha - a journey through time

Ancient Kingdoms of Odisha

Kalinga: The Beginning

The earliest known name of the region was Kalinga, mentioned in ancient texts like the Mahabharata. The Kalinga War (261 BCE) against Emperor Ashoka was a turning point in Indian history, leading to Ashoka's conversion to Buddhism.

The Kalinga kingdom was known for its maritime trade, with ancient ports connecting it to Southeast Asia. The region was famous for its skilled artisans and the Kalinga style of architecture.

Medieval Odisha

During the medieval period, the region came to be known as Odra or Udra. The powerful Ganga dynasty (11th-15th century) built magnificent temples like the Jagannath Temple in Puri and the Sun Temple at Konark.

The term "Odisha" is derived from the ancient Prakrit word "Odda Visaya" (or "Odra Visaya") mentioned in inscriptions dating back to the 10th century CE. The region was also referred to as "Orda" in Persian and Arabic accounts.

The Linguistic Evolution

The name "Odisha" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Odra" or "Odra Visaya". The ancient Prakrit word was "Odda Visaya", which gradually evolved into "Odisha" in the local Odia language. The British anglicized it to "Orissa", which remained the official name until 2011 when the state government officially changed it back to the phonetically accurate "Odisha" to better reflect the pronunciation in the Odia language.

Cultural Heritage

Jagannath Culture

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is the cultural heart of Odisha, with the annual Rath Yatra attracting millions.

Odissi Dance

One of India's classical dance forms, Odissi originated in the temples of Odisha over 2000 years ago.

Pattachitra

Traditional cloth-based scroll painting that dates back to the 5th century, depicting mythological narratives.

Modern Odisha

Today, Odisha is a vibrant state that proudly preserves its ancient heritage while embracing modernity. The name change to "Odisha" in 2011 was a significant step in reclaiming the state's linguistic identity.

The state is home to diverse ethnic groups including the Adivasi communities, each contributing to Odisha's rich cultural tapestry. The official language is Odia, one of India's classical languages with a literary tradition dating back over 1500 years.
